# Style: Authoritative

## Best Fit
Policy guidance, expert commentary, decision memos.

## Rules

### Rule: Use Strong Claims With Grounding
Description: Confidence must be evidence-backed.
Negative example: "This is the only way to do it."
Positive example: "For regulated workflows, this structure is the lowest-risk default."

### Rule: Remove Hedging That Hides Position
Description: Avoid unnecessary uncertainty language.
Negative example: "This might potentially be somewhat better."
Positive example: "This is better when the goal is repeatable, auditable output."

### Rule: State Boundaries Explicitly
Description: Authoritative voice must acknowledge scope limits.
Negative example: Universal claims without context.
Positive example: "This guidance applies to weekly operational updates, not experimental research notes."
