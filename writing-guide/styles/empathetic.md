# Style: Empathetic

## Best Fit
Onboarding, change communication, educational remediation, sensitive topics.

## Rules

### Rule: Name the Reader's Friction Clearly
Description: Show you understand the actual difficulty.
Negative example: "It is easy, just do it."
Positive example: "Most drafts fail in the first paragraph because the outcome is not stated."

### Rule: Support Without Patronizing
Description: Respect reader capability while reducing confusion.
Negative example: "Do not worry, this is simple even for beginners."
Positive example: "This is easier when you start with one sentence: what the reader gets."

### Rule: Pair Validation With Action
Description: Acknowledge challenge, then provide a concrete next step.
Negative example: "Writing is hard for everyone."
Positive example: "If your opener feels weak, rewrite it with WHO-WHAT-WHY in three lines."
