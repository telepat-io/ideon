# Style: Academic

## Best Fit
Literature synthesis, formal analysis, research-backed essays.

## Rules

### Rule: Calibrate Certainty
Description: Match claim strength to evidence quality.
Negative example: "This definitively proves..."
Positive example: "These findings suggest..."

### Rule: Keep Formality Without Inflated Complexity
Description: Formal does not require unreadable sentence construction.
Negative example: Multi-clause passive constructions that obscure action.
Positive example: Formal but controlled syntax with clear subject and verb.

### Rule: Distinguish Argument, Evidence, and Limitation
Description: Keep analytical boundaries explicit.
Negative example: Conclusions with no limitation statement.
Positive example: Claim, supporting evidence, then a concise limitation note.
