# Style: Analytical

## Best Fit
Comparisons, tradeoff analysis, postmortems, research summaries.

## Rules

### Rule: Make the Evaluation Criteria Explicit
Description: Show how conclusions are derived.
Negative example: "Option B is better."
Positive example: "Option B performs better on cost, speed, and failure recovery."

### Rule: Separate Observation From Interpretation
Description: Distinguish raw signals from conclusions.
Negative example: Treating a correlation as a causal conclusion.
Positive example: "Metric X rose after change Y; causality is plausible but not yet isolated."

### Rule: Surface Tradeoffs Early
Description: Analytical writing should not hide downside risk.
Negative example: Only upsides presented.
Positive example: "This improves speed but reduces flexibility in edge cases."
